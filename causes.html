Causes

I support a few organizations for the causes they represent and their active work in promoting these issues. I've listed below their names with a short description about why I think their work is valuable.

Free Software Foundation

Even before I knew about the FSF, I was already using their work. The FSF stands for the democratization of the software we write and use. For more than 30 years, they have led what is called the Free Software Movement -- an effort to move towards a computing future where the software that we run on our computers is not locked down or otherwise changed without our control.

This is only achievable through software 'freedom', the ability to use your software with complete control over all parts it. The FSF has been working on creating a completely free (as in freedom) operating system infrastructure under what is known as the GNU Project. Fortunately, they they have succeeded in large part.

A reasonable way to support them would be to donate to them. But that holds little value towards helping the cause they stand for. I believe, the real value lies in using, promoting and possibly writing software which gives users more freedom in using it.

Wikipedia

I doubt this needs more explanation but here is one anyway. I've known Wikipedia as long as I've known the internet. Early on, it was the site where I ended up after searching for any topic. Now, it the definitive for a wealth of information difficult to find otherwise. I am confident that it would morph into a cultural, technological and historical time vault in the future.

I can't count the number of times Wikipedia has made me feel like an expert on a topic overnight (usually before exams). It is difficult to imagine, therefore, that it is a free resource, collated by tens of thousands of knowledgeable people from around the world. It is the Library of Alexandria of our times, only wider and more accessible.

More importantly, Wikipedia represents free information - a resource as valuable as oil but probably not as dangerous for humans! The growth of Wikipedia serves as an example to what people can achieve through collaboration given the freedom to do so.
